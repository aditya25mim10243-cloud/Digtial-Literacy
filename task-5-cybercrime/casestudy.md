# Case Study: The Deceptive "KYC Update" Phishing Attack

### Overview
This case study examines a Phishing scam, which is a method where attackers masquerade as a trusted entity to steal sensitive information.

### How the Crime Happens (Step-by-Step)
1. **Initial Contact:** The victim receives an urgent SMS or email appearing to be from their bank, stating their account will be blocked unless they update their KYC (Know Your Customer) details immediately.
2. **The Hook:** The message contains a link to a "secure portal" that looks identical to the official banking website.
3. **Information Theft:** Once the victim enters their username, password, and mobile number on the fake site, the attacker captures these credentials in real-time.
4. **The Transaction:** The attacker uses the stolen credentials on the real banking site.When the bank sends an OTP (One-Time Password) to the victim's phone, the fake portal prompts the victim to enter it, allowing the attacker to authorize a fraudulent transfer.

### Targets and Consequences
College students and elderly individuals are often targeted due to high digital activity or lack of technical security awareness.The consequences include total loss of funds in the linked account, identity theft, and significant emotional stress.
